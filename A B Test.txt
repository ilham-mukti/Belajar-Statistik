A/B Test

Apakah kelompok Eksperimen yang mendapat perlakuan kinerjanya lebih baik setelah diberikan perlakuan?

The rule of thumb is as below:
1. Split the targeted population into two groups: Control (no treatment) and Experiment (gets treatment)
2. Determine the performance metrics that we want to measure, and what is the desired outcome (with treatment, we should expect Experiment group performs better than Control group)
3. Ensure both Control & Experiment groups have the same performance before treatment
4. Apply the treatment for a certain time period
5. Evaluate the result, whether if Experiment group performs better than Control group

For A/B testing, we should use two samples test that is not paired, because:
1. We want to compare two samples or two groups (Control group and Experiment group).
2. What we want to compare is the difference between Control group and Experiment group post-treatment (not paired or independent), NOT THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN pre-treatment and post-treatment (paired or dependent).

Evaluating the Result of A/B Test:
1. Pre-treatment period: Ensure that before getting the treatment, both Control & Experiment groups have the same performance
2. Post-treatment period: Prove the hypothesis of whether if Experiment group performs better (has a significantly higher performance) compared to Control group

Ringkasan:
1. Bagi data menjadi 2 kelompok, kelompok kontrol dan kelompok eksperimen.
2. Lakukan test statistik pada kelompok itu. Fungsinya untuk memastikan kedua kelompok tersebut sama/tidak siginifikan berbeda.
3. Berikan treatment pada kelompok eksperimen dan jangan berikan ke kelompok kontrol.
4. Lakukan test statistik pada kelompok tersebut, lihat ada perbedaan yang signifikan atau tidak.
5. Kalau ada berarti treatmentnya menunjukan signfikansi.
